Halls of Residence

Should I stay in Halls of Residence?

Many students decide to remain in halls of residence in second year, private halls often offer the option to live your friends and facilities such as a games room and a gym may be included in your rent. On site security is also included, which can help students feel safe during their stay.
Pros: Shorter Contract, Student Neighbours, Security, Facilities and Inclusive Bills.
Cons: Usually more expensive, often no outdoor space.

Privately Rented Accommodation

Should I stay in privately rented accommodation?

Many students opt to live in rented homes during their second year, this can often allow for more independence and can be cheaper than living in halls.
Pros: Move in over Summer, Local community, more choice of room style, might have outdoor space and usually more affordable.
Cons: Non-student neighbours and might have to travel further in.

Living at home

Should I live at home?

If you live locally, living at home could save you a lot of money. However, the cost of commuting into uni should be considered. Students can get a discount on public transport such as the Metro or Stage Coach Buses, have a look to see if any other bus or transport companies offer a similar discount. Societies usually hold socials every month, and you can still go out with friends so a social life can still be had even though you are not living with flatmates.

Timeframes

When should I have sorted my living arrangements for Second Year?

Many students get their accommodation sorted within first semester. However, you shouldn't worry as accommodation can be found all year round. Try to ensure you are living with people you get along well with. If you are struggling to find accommodation or people to live with, take a look at Castle Leasez Ticket Exchange on Facebook, as there are always people looking for additional house mates.
