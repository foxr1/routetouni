Societies are a great way to meet like-minded people and there are over 200 societies to choose from including Cheese & Wine Society, 20-Minute Society and Irish Dance Many societies offer weekly socials and activities for you to enjoy. Volunteering

Why Volunteer?

"By volunteering with Go Volunteer you can develop the skills future employers look for, help the local community and make new friends." There are a wealth of volunteering opportunities here at Newcastle including:
North East Solidarity and Teaching (N.E.S.T): empowers the refugee and asylum-seeking community in the region through a variety of formats such as one-to-one English teaching, group English classes, sports, creative arts and trips into the local area.
Helping our Homeless Society: Work to raise awareness by offering educational workshops and directly aiding Newcastle’s homeless population by distributing supplies.
Beekeeping:NUSU has bees on and off campus, and we are always looking for volunteers to help look after them. We also link up with other beekeeping folk and pollinator-friendly community projects across Newcastle to do our best to keep the stripy things abuzz. For more information on the opportunities available please visit the Go Volunteer Website

Sports Clubs

Why Join a Sports Club?

With 65 sports clubs run through your Athletic Union (AU), NUSU has an endless supply of sporting activities to help you get your game face on! With a huge variety of clubs on offer, from American Football to Water Polo, there really is something for everyone. Visit the NUSU website to browse our clubs.
Campus Leagues is our recreational sports programme created with the objective of providing sporting opportunities to all Newcastle University students – regardless of their fitness and ability. Ready for a laugh and some friendly competition? Then you’ll love our programme consisting of both team and individual activities.

Student Media

What is Student Media?

Our award-winning Student Media team is always hot on the heels for the latest coverage. The Courier Newspaper Anyone who is a student at Newcastle University can write for the Courier. Whether you want to review your favourite film, comment on worldwide issues, update students about what’s happening on campus or hold your university to account with some investigative journalism, there’s space in the Courier for everyone’s voice to be heard.
Newcastle Student RadioBroadcasting since 1998, NSR is an award-winning student radio station, the official sound of Newcastle University. NSR are online 24/7 bringing you an array of shows, created and produced by students, for students. Looking to unlock your inner potential? Well here at NSR, we offer a wide range of opportunities to learn the tricks of the radio trade, from hosting your own radio show to event organisation and live punditry. Whether it’s presenting, producing or behind the scenes roles you’re interested in, contact us to get involved today!
NUTV is NUSU Media’s high-action and fast-paced television branch. We produce content covering current affairs, entertainment, music, student talent and lifestyle, sports, and more! Have an idea for a brand new show? Come and make that show a reality, as we’re always looking for new show concepts! NUTV also aims to help bridge connections into the industry for anyone looking to pursue a media career. We are a NaSTA affiliated station, allowing us access to the workshops and industry trips the Association runs throughout the year, including the annual conference at Easter.
